Reflection on Making Chocolates from Stephen Durfee from Dandelion Chocolate

October 8, 2024

Today, I had the opportunity to hear from Stephen Durfee from Dandelion Chocolate and learn about the process of making chocolates. It was an amazing experience to see how the chocolates are made and to taste the different flavors. I also had the chance to talk to Stephen Durfee, the CEO of Dandelion Chocolate, and ask him about his journey to starting his own business. It was inspiring to hear about his passion for chocolate and his commitment to using high-quality ingredients. I learned a lot from this experience and am grateful for the opportunity to visit Dandelion Chocolate. Stephen Durfee's lecture was a fascinating dive into the art and science of chocolate making. With a background as the Executive Pastry Chef at The French Laundry and over 20 years of teaching at the Culinary Institute of America, Durfee now leads the chocolate curriculum at Dandelion Chocolate in San Francisco. In my cooking class, he walked us through the process of turning cacao seeds into fine chocolate, using traditional tools like stone grinders. We even got to taste three different types of chocolate he helped create, sourced from Tumaco, Colombia, Madagascar, and Uganda. Durfee highlighted how different regions produce unique flavors and taught us to savor chocolate slowly, letting the cacao butter melt in your mouth for the full experience. It was an eye-opening session that made me appreciate the craftsmanship behind every piece of chocolate. Augmented fermentation will alter the process of making chocolate.
